Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

Thomas Jefferson
n 1823 Jefferson wrote that the other members of the committee "unanimously pressed on myself alone to undertake the draught [sic]. I consented; I drew it; but before I reported it to the committee I communicated it separately to Dr. Franklin and Mr. Adams requesting their corrections. . . I then wrote a fair copy, reported it to the committee, and from them, unaltered to the Congress.

Thomas Jefferson
Primary author of the Declaration and later the 3rd President of the United States.
John Adams
A strong advocate for independence and later the 2nd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
Renowned inventor, diplomat, and one of the most influential Founding Fathers.
John Hancock
President of the Second Continental Congress, famous for his large and bold signature.
Samuel Adams
Cousin of John Adams and a leader in the push for American independence.
Richard Henry Lee
Introduced the resolution calling for independence from Britain.
Roger Sherman
Helped draft the Declaration and was a member of the committee that worked on it.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The only Catholic signer and one of the wealthiest men in the colonies.
Robert Morris
A financier who helped fund the Revolutionary War.
