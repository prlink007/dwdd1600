ABOUT THE CREATORS

Paul Lingenbrink

"Hi! Im Paul!"

Paul Lingenbrink is a graphic designer by trade and a film enthusiast at heart. With a background in visual storytelling and a BFA in Graphic Design, Paul has always been fascinated by the power of imagery, composition, and pacing — whether in a poster or on the big screen. His passion for cinema led him to study film theory and narrative structure at the Pacific School of Visual Arts, where he explored everything from classic auteur filmmaking to contemporary indie cinema. Over the years, Paul has combined his love for design and film into various creative projects, including short film storyboarding, poster design for local film festivals, and most recently, the creation of CritiqueCinema. He built this space to help others dive deeper into the art of film — not just to watch, but to truly see.

Ely Vargas

"Ummm, What do you want me to say?"

Ely Vargas, Paul’s husband and partner in cinephile adventures, brings a whole different flavor to CritiqueCinema. While his academic background is in Computer Science, his heart has always been with the movies. A true film fanatic, Ely can quote Tarantino and debate Kubrick with the best of them — all while troubleshooting a broken website or optimizing code behind the scenes. His love for cinema started with late-night horror marathons and has since grown into a deep appreciation for everything from foreign arthouse gems to classic Hollywood. Watching and dissecting films together has become a shared ritual between Paul and Ely, blending their unique perspectives — Paul’s as a designer, Ely’s as a techie — into something creative, thoughtful, and fun. Ely helps bring CritiqueCinema to life not only through his tech savvy, but with his infectious enthusiasm for film.

And of Course… Unico the Cat

"MEOW!"

Rounding out the CritiqueCinema crew is Unico — the true star of the living room. As Paul and Ely’s fluffy companion and unofficial emotional support cinephile, Unico may not have a degree in film studies, but he does have a sixth sense for cozy movie nights. Whether it's curling up during a slow-burn drama or pouncing during a high-stakes action scene, Unico is always part of the cinematic experience. He’s particularly fond of warm blankets, opening credits, and anything with dramatic music. While he might sleep through most of the third act, his snuggles and purring approval are an essential part of our film-watching ritual.

A Few of Our Favorite Film Terms

Visual Storytelling
Paul’s design playground — using imagery, color, and layout to convey a narrative without a single word.
Director's Cut
Ely's preferred version — often longer, weirder, and with all the extra scenes studios didn’t want you to see.
Catnap Cut
Unico's personal favorite — the part of the movie perfect for a cozy nap, usually during slow dialogue or credits.
